# AI-Native OS — Explained Simply

## The Short Version

We're building software that lets AI agents run your product development process. Think of it as a project management tool — but instead of humans doing all the organizing, AI agents do it automatically.

## Why Does This Exist?

Every software company has the same problem: too much feedback, too many ideas, not enough time.

Customer support tickets pile up. Meeting notes get lost. The team has 200 feature requests and no clear way to decide what to build next. Someone has to read through everything, figure out what matters, organize it, assign work, track progress, update stakeholders... It's exhausting and most of it is busywork.

## What If AI Did the Busywork?

That's what this is. Here's how it works:

### Step 1: Feedback Comes In (Insights)

Customer emails, support tickets, Slack messages, meeting notes, user research — all of it flows into the system. The AI reads everything. Humans can't read 500 support tickets a day. AI can.

### Step 2: AI Finds the Patterns (Bets)

The AI groups related feedback together and identifies what's important. "50 customers are asking for the same thing" or "this bug is affecting our biggest accounts." When there's enough signal, it proposes a **bet** — an experiment the team should try. We call it a "bet" because every product decision is a gamble until you see how users react.

### Step 3: Work Gets Done (Tasks)

The AI breaks each bet into specific tasks and starts working on them. No one needs to sit in a project board dragging cards around.

### Step 4: Humans Steer

Throughout all of this, the team can see what's happening in real-time. They can course-correct the AI, reprioritize, or jump in and do things manually. But they don't have to — the AI runs on its own by default.

Think of it like a self-driving car: it drives itself, but you can always grab the wheel.

## What Makes This Different From Jira, Linear, Asana, etc.?

- **AI agents are the main users**, not humans. The whole system is designed for AI to operate. Humans get a dashboard to see what's happening and steer when needed.
- **It's open source.** You can download it and run it yourself for free. No vendor lock-in.
- **It's real-time.** Everything updates live. No refreshing, no waiting.

## The Business Model

The software itself is free and open source. We make money by hosting it for companies — we handle the servers, security, and compliance (especially important for European companies under GDPR). Think of it like WordPress: the software is free, but lots of companies pay someone to host and manage it.

## The Bigger Picture

This product development tool is just the beginning. We're planning to build a whole suite of AI-powered business tools — a CRM, a meeting note-taker, and more. Each one will be open source and designed the same way: AI agents do the work, humans steer. They can all run independently or together as one unified platform.
